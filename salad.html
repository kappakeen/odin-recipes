Baja Fresh Taco Salad at home

Fish tacos are a perennial beach favorite. Around since the ‘50s, they’re a laid-back meal that’s often mistakenly considered light. Ditch the corn tortillas and the heavy batter on the fish but load up on vegetables and fish tacos become a healthy meal.

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • ¾ tsp. each ground cumin and coriander
  • ½ tsp. each kosher salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper
  • ¾ cup canola oil
  • 3 egg whites, beaten
  • 2 Tbsp. water 1 lb. tilapia fillets
  • 6 cups thinly sliced green cabbage
  • 1 cucumber, seeded and diced
  • 1 cup halved cherry or grape tomatoes
  • 1 cup sliced radishes
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro leaves
  1. For the fish, whisk together cornmeal, cumin, coriander, salt, black pepper, and cayenne in a bowl.
  2. Heat oil in a large sauté pan over medium-high.
  3. Combine egg whites and water in a small bowl.
  4. Cut fillets into ½ x 2-inch strips. Dredge strips in cornmeal mixture; dip into egg white mixture. Roll strips in cornmeal mixture a second time. Fry fish, in batches, in hot oil until golden; transfer to a paper-towel-lined plate.
  5. For the salad, toss cabbage, cucumber, tomatoes, radishes, and cilantro in a large bowl. Divide salad among four plates, and top each with fish. Serve salads with Creamy Jalapeño Dressing.
